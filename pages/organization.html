Our
Organization.

REACH is led by a small board of professionals — educators, attorneys, healthcare leaders — who share a commitment to metro Atlanta's student-athletes.

Founder

Brian Q. Lee

Brian Q. Lee founded REACH after a season-ending injury and the loss of his mother to breast cancer reshaped his senior year at Westlake High School. A 2005 Wendy's High School Heisman Georgia State finalist, Brian made the jump to college without a mentor — and decided no scholar from his community should have to.

REACH awarded its first scholarships in 2020 and 2021, paused during COVID when his daughter was born prematurely, and is re-launching for the 2026–2027 school year.

Brian lives in Atlanta with his family and continues to lead REACH alongside a board of professionals committed to metro Atlanta's student-athletes.

Kenneth A. Mitchell
Director

Kenneth is a dedicated social justice reform advocate. A graduate of Georgetown University and American University Washington College of Law, he is now a Partner at Giddens, Mitchell & Associates, P.C., continuing the work he led as an Assistant District Attorney for Fulton County, where he helped overhaul the justice system for youth and adults. A former stand-out athlete at Georgetown and Westlake High School, Kenneth serves on the South Fulton Democrats and the executive board of his Omega Psi Phi Fraternity, Inc. chapter.

Aria J. Lee
Director

As one of the youngest and only Black female members of the leadership team at a healthcare-technology startup, Aria is passionate about minority students and professionals “having a seat at the table.” A dedicated youth education advocate, she is a former INROADS Alumni Mentor and has volunteered extensively with Junior Achievement. Aria spent several years as a Manager with top-tier consulting firms, holds a B.S. from Howard University and an M.B.A. from Georgia State, and is a proud member of Delta Sigma Theta Sorority, Inc.
